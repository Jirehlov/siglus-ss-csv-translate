# Rhetoric Audit

Use this file during review.

The goal is not generic beautification. The goal is to catch lines whose literal meaning survived but whose extra force, reveal timing, or character performance was flattened.

## Recheck These Layers

- slang, rough talk, youth talk, or subculture jargon
- wordplay, nickname play, sound-play, or deliberate misnaming
- speech tics, pet sounds, catchphrase syllables, or recurring interjections
- acronym jokes, wrong expansions, or correction punchlines
- cross-line setup-payoff chains that only land when adjacent replies are read together
- allusion, mock-formal phrasing, role-play diction, or parody self-presentation
- regional or local spoken coloring
- lyrical memory passages or introspective monologue clusters whose risk lies in tone thinning
- spoiler-sensitive lines where later knowledge could leak backward into earlier-scene wording

## Failure Mode To Avoid

A translation can be factually correct yet still fail because it becomes neutral, flat, over-explained, or prematurely revealing.

## Review Questions

For each difficult line, ask:

- What is the line doing socially, emotionally, comedically, or narratively beyond literal meaning?
- If I remove the slang, joke, locality signal, or ambiguity, does the scene lose something essential?
- Does the Chinese still perform that function?
- If this line is part of a setup and payoff, does the payoff still land one or two lines later?
- If a nickname or correction grows out of a nearby sound or mishearing, is that link still audible or intuitive in Chinese?
- Did I accidentally explain something earlier than the story currently allows?
- If not, can I restore the missing layer without damaging faithfulness?

Also ask this for nearby unflagged lines when the scene enters memory narration, emotional self-questioning, or other dense reflective prose.
